JCT-PKI online help

Welcome to the online help of the JCrypTool Public-Key Infrastructure (JCT-PKI). The JCT-PKI plugin shows the basic functions and processes of a public-key infrastructure. On the one hand, this online help contains detailed explanations on the parts of the plug-in. On the other hand, there is profound information on the topics of PKI and digital certificates.

About this plug-in

The JCT-PKI plug-in was created during a project at the University of Applied Sciences Upper Austria, Hagenberg Campus. It should explain the basic functions of a PKI. The shown processes do not claim to be complete and have been simplified to ensure better understandability. The goal is to teach the user a basic understanding of the need of PKIs and which processes are tied with the creation and the revocation of a certificate. Some concepts which are important for the operator of a PKI but do not have any significance for the user are omitted to make it easier to understand. A detailed view of all aspects on PKIs would also be far too complex for a project that needs to be done during one term and for such a basic educational program.

Design of the online help

In Introduction to the problems you get an overview on the many problems that a PKI tries to solve. Digital signatures and certificates explains the concepts used by a PKI to solve the stated problems. PKI processes describes the activities necessary to create a certificate, to check a signature and to revoke a certificate. The chapters RA and CA under "Roles of a PKI" explain two very important parts of a PKI: the registration authority and certification authority. In X.509 and PKIX you find more information on the standards used to describe digital certificates.
