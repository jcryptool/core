1 The Shamir’s Secret Sharing Algorithm

Shamir’s Secret Sharing Algorithm is a developed in 1979 by Adi Shamir secret sharing method which it is possible to keep a secret among several people with a certain subset of these individuals needed to reconstruct the secret.

Let n and t be natural numbers. In a (n,t)-Secret-Sharing-Protocol is shared the secret to n people. Every person has a share of the secret. If t of these people get together they can reconstruct the secret. If less than t together this part secret information they can not obtain relevant information about the secret. This principle can be compared with a treasure map that is distributed to several people.Only when all parts are merged the treasure map then you will find the treasure.

Shamir's algorithm is based on the Lagrage-polynomial and is an ein (n,t)-(n, t)-Secret Sharing Protocol. All calculations are carried out in ℤ∕pℤ .

The dealer wants to distribute a secret s ∈ ℤ∕pℤ .

  1. He chooses secret elements aj ∈ ℤ∕pℤ,1 ≤ j ≤ t - 1 and generated the polynomial form

    a(X) = s + ∑ ajXj, 1 ≤ j ≤ t - 1.

    The polynomial of degree ≤ t - 1.

  2. The dealer calculated the shares yi = a(xi),1 ≤ i ≤ n.
  3. The dealer gives every i-th Shareholder its Share yi,1 ≤ i ≤ n. The secret is the constant term a(0) of the polynomial a(X).

To reconstruct the secret, we assume that t Shareholder work together. Their shares were yi = a(xi),1 ≤ i ≤ t. Here a(X) is the polynomial form (1). It is

wi = yi ∏  (xj - X) ∕ (xj - xi), 1 ≤ j ≤ t,j≠i.

and

a(X) = ∑ wi, 1 ≤ i ≤ t.

Displayed in a formula

a(X) = ∑ yi ∏  (xj - X) ∕ (xj - xi), 1 ≤ i,j ≤ t,j≠i.

Since the coefficient a(0) is the secret we calculate

s = a(0) = ∑ yi ∏  (xj) ∕ (xj - xi), 1 ≤ i,j ≤ t,j≠i.

This formula is used by the shareholders in order to construct the secret.

We want to show this with an example.

Example Let n = 5,t = 3. The dealer choose p = 17,xi = i,1 ≤ i ≤ 5. Let the secret s = 5. The dealer chooses the secret coefficients a1 = 7,a2 = 11. Thus we obtain the polynomial

a(X) = 5 + 7X + 11X2.

The shares are therefore y1 = a(1) = 6,y2 = a(2) = 12,y3 = a(3) = 6,y4 = a(4) = 5,y5 = a(5) = 9. These are now being distributed to the shareholders. The shareholder 1,2 and 3 are now used to reconstruct the secret. It is calculated the Lagrange polynomial, and the shares were multipled with yi

wi = yi ∏  (xj) ∕ (xj - xi) 1 ≤ i,j ≤ 3,j≠i.

It is clear for w1 = 4 + 9x + 6x2,w2 = 3 + 2x + 11x2,w3 = 15 + 13x + 11x2. Now we compute the sum of the wis and so we get the original polynomial

∑ wi = 5 + 7x + 11x2, 1 ≤ i ≤ 3.

The coefficient a0 = 5 is our sought secret s.
