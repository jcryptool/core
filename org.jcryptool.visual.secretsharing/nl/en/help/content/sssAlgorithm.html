Shamir's Secret Sharing Algorithm

Shamir's Secret Sharing algorithm was developed in 1979 by Adi Shamir as a secret sharing method which allows to distribute shares of a secret among several people, and only a subset of them is needed to reconstruct the secret.You can compare this with a safe where 3 of 5 different keys are sufficient to open it.

Let n and t be natural numbers with t ≤ n. In a (n,t)-Secret-Sharing-Protocol is shared the secret to n people. Every person has a share of the secret. If t of these people get together they can reconstruct the secret. If less than t together this part secret information they can not obtain relevant information about the secret. This principle is much better than cutting a treasure map into pieces and distribute the pieces to several people.

Shamir's algorithm uses the Lagrange interpolation in order to reconstruct the polynomial. All calculations are carried out in ℤ∕pℤ .

Given, the dealer is the one who wants to distribute a secret s ∈ ℤ∕pℤ.

  1. He chooses secret elements aj ∈ ℤ∕pℤ, 1 ≤ j ≤ t - 1 and generates the polynomial

    P(x) = s + ∑ ajXj, 1 ≤ j ≤ t - 1.

    The polynomial is of degree ≤ t - 1.

  2. The dealer calculated the shares yi = P(xi),1 ≤ i ≤ n.
  3. The dealer gives every i-th shareholder its share yi,1 ≤ i ≤ n. The secret is the constant term a0 of the polynomial P(x).

To reconstruct the secret, we assume that t shareholders work together. Their shares were yi = P(xi), 1 ≤ i ≤ t. Here, P(x) is the polynomial from step 1 above (1). It is

wi = yi ∏  (xj - X) ∕ (xj - xi) mod p, 1 ≤ j ≤ t,j≠i.

and

P(x) = ∑ wi mod p, 1 ≤ i ≤ t.

Combined in one formula

P(x) = ∑ yi ∏  (xj - X) ∕ (xj - xi) mod p, 1 ≤ i,j ≤ t,j≠i.

Since the coefficient a0 is the secret, we calculate

s = a0 = ∑ yi ∏  (xj) ∕ (xj - xi), 1 ≤ i,j ≤ t,j≠i.

This formula is used by the shareholders in order to reconstruct the secret.

We want to show this with an example.


Example Let n = 5, t = 3. The dealer chooses p = 17, xi = i, 1 ≤ i ≤ 5. Let the secret s = 5. The dealer chooses the secret coefficients a1 = 7, a2 = 11. Thus we obtain the polynomial

P(x) = 5 + 7x + 11x2 mod p.

Using 5 random values for x, the shares are therefore y1 = P(1) = 6, y2 = P(2) = 12, y3 = P(3) = 6, y4 = P(4) = 5, y5 = P(5) = 9. These are being distributed to the shareholders. The shareholders 1, 2, and 3 now come together to reconstruct the secret. The Lagrange polynomial is calculated, and the shares were multiplied with yi:

wi = yi ∏  (xj) ∕ (xj - xi) 1 ≤ i,j ≤ 3, j≠i.

It is clear for w1 = 4 + 9x + 6x2, w2 = 3 + 2x + 11x2, w3 = 15 + 13x + 11x2. Now we compute the sum of the wi and so get the original polynomial

∑ wi = 5 + 7x + 11x2, 1 ≤ i ≤ 3.

The coefficient a0 = 5 is our sought secret s.
