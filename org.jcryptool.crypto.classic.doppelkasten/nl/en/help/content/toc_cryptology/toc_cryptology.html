Double box cipher

Double Box Cipher The double box cipher or "two-square cipher" (German: "Doppelkasten"), was a German modification of the Playfair cipher. The algorithm encrypts the plaintext twice with two different Playfair tables, and introducea splitting of the plaintext. It was deployed in German communications in 1941-1944 in World War II, and broken in Bletchley Park nearly in the same time span.
More details can be found in the further reading section.

Classification

The Double box cipher is based on digraph substitution. Like with any classical cipher heavily used in military, there are many modifications and addition to the basic algorithm.
